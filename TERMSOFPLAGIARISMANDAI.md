# Terms of Plagiarism & AI 

## Hong Kong Institute of Information Technology (HKIIT) 

### Effective Date: `2026-03-01` 

 

#### 1. Introduction 

The Hong Kong Institute of Information Technology ("HKIIT," "the Institute," "we," "us," or "our") is dedicated to fostering an environment of academic excellence, integrity, and original thought. All members of the HKIIT community – students, faculty, and staff – are expected to uphold the highest standards of intellectual honesty in all academic and professional endeavors. 

This policy outlines HKIIT's definitions of plagiarism and the acceptable and unacceptable uses of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools in academic work. It aims to clarify expectations, promote responsible scholarship, and ensure fairness across all courses and programs. Violations of this policy constitute academic misconduct and will be subject to disciplinary action. 

 

#### 2. Scope 

This policy applies to all academic work submitted or presented by students at HKIIT, including but not limited to assignments, projects, reports, essays, theses, dissertations, presentations, code, data analysis, and examinations. It also guides faculty and staff in their roles as educators, researchers, and mentors. 

 

#### 3. Definitions 

3.1. Plagiarism: Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's ideas, words, data, or work as one's own without proper acknowledgment. This includes, but is not limited to: * Direct Copying: Reproducing text, code, images, or other materials verbatim from a source without using quotation marks (or appropriate formatting for code) and citing the source. * Paraphrasing without Attribution: Rephrasing someone else's ideas, arguments, or information in your own words without citing the original source. * Summarizing without Attribution: Condensing a source's main points without citing the original source. * Using Ideas without Attribution: Incorporating concepts, theories, data, or methodologies from a source without acknowledging the original author. * Submitting Another's Work: Presenting work prepared by another person (e.g., a peer, a tutor, an online service) as your own. * Self-Plagiarism: Submitting substantial portions of your own previous work (e.g., from another course, a prior degree) without obtaining permission from the relevant instructor(s) and citing the original work. * Improper Collaboration: Collaborating on an assignment beyond what is permitted by the instructor and presenting the work as solely your own. 

3.2. Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools: AI tools refer to any software, application, or system that can generate content, code, data, or provide sophisticated analysis based on user prompts. This includes, but is not limited to, large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Google Bard, Microsoft Copilot, image generators (e.g., Midjourney, DALL-E), code generation tools (e.g., GitHub Copilot), and advanced data analysis or summarization tools. 

3.3. AI-Generated Content: Any text, code, images, data, or other academic material produced partially or wholly by an AI tool. 

 

#### 4. Policy on Plagiarism 

HKIIT maintains a zero-tolerance policy for plagiarism. All submitted work must be the result of your own original thought, research, and expression. When you draw upon the work of others, you must acknowledge those sources clearly and accurately using an appropriate citation style (e.g., APA, IEEE, MLA, Chicago, as specified by your instructor or department). 

Responsibility: It is your responsibility to understand what constitutes plagiarism and to avoid it. Ignorance of citation practices or this policy is not an acceptable excuse. 

Academic Integrity: Plagiarism undermines the integrity of academic qualifications and the learning process. It deprives you of the opportunity to develop critical thinking and research skills. 

 

#### 5. Policy on the Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools 

HKIIT recognizes the evolving nature of AI tools and their potential benefits for learning and research. However, the responsible and ethical use of AI is paramount to maintaining academic integrity. 

5.1. General Principle: Transparency and Instructor Discretion The use of AI tools in academic work at HKIIT is generally prohibited unless explicitly permitted and properly acknowledged. The specific policy on AI tool usage may vary by course, assignment, or instructor. It is your responsibility to clarify the instructor's specific guidelines for each assignment. 

5.2. Permitted Uses of AI Tools (with proper attribution and instructor permission): When an instructor permits the use of AI tools, it must be for legitimate academic purposes and transparently acknowledged. Examples of potentially permissible uses (always subject to instructor approval) include: * Brainstorming and Idea Generation: To explore initial ideas or outlines, provided the final work is your original synthesis and expression. * Grammar, Spelling, and Style Enhancement: For proofreading and refining language, similar to using a grammar checker, provided the core content and ideas remain your own. * Summarization of Complex Texts: To understand the main points of a document, provided you verify the accuracy of the summary and cite the original source, not the AI tool. * Code Generation or Debugging Assistance: To generate initial code snippets or assist in debugging, provided you thoroughly understand the code, test it, modify it as needed, and clearly attribute the AI tool's contribution. * Data Analysis (with human oversight): For initial data processing or pattern identification, provided the analysis is critically reviewed, interpreted, and verified by you. 

Crucial Requirement for Permitted Use: In all cases where AI tools are permitted, you must clearly and specifically attribute their use within your submitted work. This attribution should detail: * Which AI tool was used (e.g., ChatGPT-4, Google Bard). * When it was used. * How it was used (e.g., "AI was used to generate an initial outline for Section 2," or "AI was used to suggest alternative phrasing for paragraphs 3-5," or "AI generated the initial Python script for data parsing, which was then modified and verified by the author"). * The specific prompts used, if relevant to understanding the AI's contribution. 

5.3. Prohibited Uses of AI Tools (Academic Misconduct): The following uses of AI tools are considered academic misconduct and are strictly prohibited: * Submitting AI-Generated Content as Your Own Original Work: Presenting any AI-generated text, code, images, or other material as if it were entirely your original creation, without acknowledgment. * Using AI to Circumvent Learning Objectives: Employing AI tools in a way that bypasses the intended learning outcomes of an assignment (e.g., using AI to write an essay when the objective is to develop writing skills, using AI to solve complex problems when the objective is problem-solving). * Generating Entire Assignments: Using AI to produce the bulk or entirety of any assignment (e.g., essays, reports, code, data analysis interpretations). * Using AI During Examinations or Quizzes: Employing AI tools during invigilated examinations, quizzes, or any other assessment where external resources are not permitted. * Misrepresenting AI-Generated Content: Falsely claiming that AI-generated content is your own work, or intentionally concealing the use of AI. * Using AI to Generate Responses to Instructor-Specific Questions: Using AI to generate answers to questions that require your personal reflection, critical analysis, or specific knowledge gained from the course. 

 

#### 6. Responsibilities 

6.1. Student Responsibilities: * Understand and adhere to this Plagiarism & AI Policy and any specific instructions from your instructors regarding AI tool usage. * Ensure all submitted work is your own original effort and properly acknowledges all sources, including AI tools if permitted. * Seek clarification from your instructor if you are unsure about the appropriate use of AI tools for a particular assignment. * Be aware that AI detection tools are increasingly sophisticated, but their results are not definitive. Your understanding and ability to explain your work remain critical. 

6.2. Faculty and Instructor Responsibilities: * Clearly communicate their expectations regarding plagiarism and the use of AI tools for each course and assignment. * Educate students on proper citation practices and responsible AI usage. * Design assignments that promote critical thinking, creativity, and original work, making it more difficult to rely solely on AI. * Investigate suspected cases of plagiarism or unauthorized AI use and report them according to HKIIT procedures. 

 

#### 7. Reporting and Investigation Procedures 

Suspected violations of this Plagiarism & AI Policy will be handled in accordance with HKIIT's established procedures for academic misconduct. 

Initial Review: An instructor who suspects plagiarism or unauthorized AI use will conduct an initial review of the evidence. 

Student Notification: The student will be informed of the alleged violation and provided with an opportunity to respond and explain the situation. 

Departmental Review: If the instructor determines that a violation has likely occurred, the case may be referred to the relevant Head of Department or Academic Dean for further review. 

Disciplinary Committee: Serious or repeat offenses may be referred to a formal academic disciplinary committee. 

Due Process: Throughout the process, students will be afforded due process, including the right to present their case and appeal decisions. 

 

#### 8. Consequences of Violations 

Violations of this Plagiarism & AI Policy are serious academic offenses and will result in disciplinary action, which may include: 

A formal warning. 

Mandatory academic integrity training. 

Resubmission of the assignment with a penalty. 

A failing grade for the assignment. 

A failing grade for the course. 

Suspension from the Institute for a specified period. 

Expulsion from the Institute. 

Revocation of a degree or certificate. 

The severity of the penalty will depend on factors such as the nature and extent of the misconduct, the student's intent, previous academic integrity violations, and the impact on academic fairness.
