# Community Guidelines 

## Hong Kong Institute of Information Technology (HKIIT) 

### Effective Date: `2026-03-01`

 

#### 1. Introduction 

Welcome to the Hong Kong Institute of Information Technology (HKIIT) community! These Community Guidelines outline the expectations for conduct and interaction for all members of the HKIIT community, including students, faculty, staff, alumni, and visitors, whether on campus, at Institute-sponsored events, or on our digital platforms (websites, learning management systems, social media channels, forums, etc.). 

Our aim is to foster a vibrant, inclusive, and respectful environment where everyone feels safe, valued, and empowered to learn, collaborate, and innovate. By being a part of HKIIT, you agree to uphold these guidelines. 

 

#### 2. Our Core Principles 

These guidelines are built upon the following core principles: 

Respect: Treat all individuals with dignity, courtesy, and empathy, regardless of their background, beliefs, or identity. 

Integrity: Uphold the highest standards of honesty, academic integrity, and ethical conduct in all interactions and activities. 

Inclusivity: Actively promote an environment where diversity is celebrated, and all voices are heard and respected. 

Safety: Contribute to a physical and digital environment that is free from harassment, discrimination, bullying, and any form of harm. 

Responsibility: Take ownership of your actions and their impact on the community, and use Institute resources responsibly. 

 

#### 3. General Conduct Expectations 

All members of the HKIIT community are expected to: 

3.1. Be Respectful and Courteous: * Engage in constructive discussions and debates, even when opinions differ. * Use appropriate language and tone in all communications, both verbal and written. * Respect personal boundaries and privacy. 

3.2. Promote Inclusivity and Diversity: * Welcome and respect individuals from all backgrounds, including but not limited to race, ethnicity, nationality, gender, sexual orientation, religion, age, disability, and socioeconomic status. * Actively challenge and report any form of discrimination or exclusion. 

3.3. Maintain Academic and Professional Integrity: * Adhere to HKIIT's Academic Integrity Policy (e.g., no cheating, plagiarism, or unauthorized collaboration). * Be honest and truthful in all academic and administrative dealings. * Conduct yourselves professionally in classes, meetings, and all Institute-related activities. 

3.4. Ensure Safety and Well-being: * Refrain from any form of harassment, intimidation, bullying (physical or cyber), or threats of violence. * Do not engage in any activity that endangers yourself or others. * Report any safety concerns or suspicious activities to the appropriate authorities. 

3.5. Respect Property and Resources: * Treat Institute property, facilities, and equipment with care and respect. * Use IT resources (networks, computers, software) ethically and in accordance with HKIIT's IT Acceptable Use Policy. * Respect the intellectual property of others (refer to HKIIT's Copyright Guidelines). 

3.6. Comply with Laws and Policies: * Abide by all applicable laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. * Adhere to all HKIIT policies, rules, and regulations. 

 

#### 4. Online and Digital Conduct 

Given HKIIT's focus, special attention is paid to digital interactions: 

4.1. Be Mindful of Your Digital Footprint: Remember that anything you post online can be widely shared and may reflect on you and the Institute. 4.2. Respect Privacy Online: * Do not share personal or private information about others without their explicit consent. * Do not engage in "doxing" (publishing private information about an individual without their consent). * Be cautious about sharing your own private information. 4.3. No Cyberbullying or Harassment: This includes, but is not limited to, sending threatening messages, spreading rumors, posting embarrassing photos/videos, or creating fake profiles to demean others. 4.4. Appropriate Content: * Do not post or share content that is illegal, obscene, defamatory, hateful, or promotes discrimination. * Avoid spamming, phishing, or other malicious online activities. 4.5. Responsible Use of Institute Platforms: * Use HKIIT's learning platforms (e.g., Moodle), email systems, and communication channels for their intended academic and administrative purposes. * Do not share course materials, lecture recordings, or proprietary Institute content outside of authorized channels without explicit permission (refer to Copyright Guidelines). * Maintain respectful and constructive dialogue in online forums, discussion boards, and chat groups. 4.6. Identify Yourself Accurately: Do not impersonate others or create misleading online identities. 

 

#### 5. Representing HKIIT 

When you are participating in Institute-related activities, wearing HKIIT apparel, or identifying yourself as an HKIIT member, your actions may be perceived as representing the Institute. We encourage you to: 

Act as a positive ambassador for HKIIT. 

Be mindful that your public conduct can impact the Institute's reputation. 

 

#### 6. Reporting Violations 

If you witness or experience a violation of these Community Guidelines, we encourage you to report it. HKIIT is committed to addressing concerns promptly and fairly. 

For Academic Misconduct: Contact your course instructor, department head, or the relevant academic office. 

For Harassment, Discrimination, or Bullying: Contact Student Affairs (for students) or Human Resources (for faculty/staff). 

For IT-related Misconduct or Cyberbullying: Contact the IT Helpdesk or the relevant administrative office. 

For General Concerns or Unsure Who to Contact: Reach out to Student Affairs or the General Administration Office. 

All reports will be handled with discretion, and HKIIT will take reasonable steps to protect the privacy of individuals involved, consistent with its obligation to investigate and address the matter. Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith is strictly prohibited. 

 

#### 7. Consequences of Violations 

Violations of these Community Guidelines may result in disciplinary action, which can range from warnings and mandatory training to suspension, expulsion from the Institute (for students), or termination of employment (for faculty/staff). Severe violations may also lead to legal action, consistent with Hong Kong law. 

HKIIT reserves the right to remove any content or restrict access to its platforms or facilities for individuals who violate these guidelines. 

 

#### 8. Review and Updates 

HKIIT reserves the right to review and update these Community Guidelines periodically to ensure they remain relevant and effective. Any significant changes will be communicated to the community.
