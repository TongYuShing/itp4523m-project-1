# Privacy Policy 

## Hong Kong Institute of Information Technology (HKIIT) 

### Effective Date: `2026-03-01`

 

#### 1. Introduction 

The Hong Kong Institute of Information Technology ("HKIIT," "the Institute," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of its students, prospective students, alumni, faculty, staff, researchers, visitors, and other individuals with whom we interact. This Privacy Policy outlines how HKIIT collects, uses, stores, discloses, and protects your personal data in accordance with the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486) of Hong Kong (the "PDPO") and other applicable laws and regulations. 

By accessing or using our website, online platforms, educational services, facilities, or by providing your personal data to us, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. 

 

#### 2. Scope of this Policy 

This Privacy Policy applies to all personal data collected, processed, and held by HKIIT, whether in electronic, written, or other forms. It covers: 

Our official website and associated sub-domains. 

Our online learning platforms (e.g., Learning Management Systems). 

Applications for admission, employment, or other services. 

Registration for courses, events, or programs. 

Communications with the Institute (email, phone, in-person). 

Use of Institute facilities and IT systems. 

 

#### 3. Types of Personal Data Collected 

HKIIT collects various types of personal data depending on your relationship with the Institute. This may include, but is not limited to: 

Personal Identification Information: Name, date of birth, gender, nationality, Hong Kong Identity Card number (or passport/travel document number), photograph, contact details (address, email, phone number). 

Academic Information: Educational background, qualifications, academic transcripts, examination results, student ID number, course enrollment details, attendance records, disciplinary records, graduation information. 

Employment Information (for staff/faculty): Employment history, qualifications, payroll details, performance reviews, emergency contact information. 

Financial Information: Payment details (e.g., bank account numbers for fees/salaries, credit card information for transactions), scholarship applications, financial aid records. 

Health Information: Medical conditions, special educational needs, disability status (collected only where relevant and with appropriate consent, for purposes such as providing accommodations or emergency assistance). 

Technical and Usage Data: IP address, browser type and version, device information, operating system, browsing patterns, login data, cookies (see Section 10). 

Other Information: Information provided in surveys, feedback, enquiries, CCTV footage for security purposes on campus, and any other information you voluntarily provide to us. 

 

#### 4. How Personal Data is Collected 

We collect personal data through various means: 

Directly from You: When you apply for admission, register for a course, enroll as a student, apply for a job, fill out forms, participate in surveys, communicate with us via email, phone, or in person, or use our online services. 

Automatically: Through your interaction with our website and online platforms, via cookies and other tracking technologies (see Section 10). 

From Third Parties: 

Previous educational institutions (e.g., for academic transcripts). 

Referees (e.g., for admissions or employment). 

Government bodies or regulatory authorities (where legally required or permitted). 

Service providers (e.g., payment gateways, event organizers). 

Publicly available sources. 

 

#### 5. Purposes of Data Collection and Use (Data Protection Principle 1 & 3) 

HKIIT collects and uses your personal data for the following legitimate purposes: 

Admissions and Enrollment: Processing applications, verifying qualifications, conducting interviews, and managing the admission process. 

Academic Administration: Enrolling students, maintaining academic records, managing course registration, tracking attendance, administering examinations, issuing transcripts and certificates, and managing graduation. 

Educational Services: Providing teaching, learning resources, academic support, library services, and facilitating educational activities. 

Student Support and Welfare: Providing counseling, career services, disability support, managing student accommodation, and emergency contact. 

Financial Administration: Processing tuition fees, payments, scholarships, financial aid, and payroll (for staff). 

Communication: Sending important notices, updates, newsletters, event invitations, and responding to your enquiries. 

Marketing and Outreach (with consent): Promoting HKIIT's programs, events, and activities, and engaging with prospective students and alumni. You will have the option to opt-in or opt-out of such communications. 

Alumni Relations: Maintaining contact with alumni, organizing alumni events, and soliciting support for the Institute. 

Employment: Managing recruitment, employment contracts, performance, and human resources administration. 

Research and Statistical Analysis: Conducting institutional research, surveys, and statistical analysis to improve our services, academic programs, and operational efficiency. This data will be anonymized or aggregated where possible. 

Safety and Security: Ensuring the safety and security of our campus, facilities, staff, and students, including through CCTV surveillance and access control systems. 

Compliance and Legal Obligations: Fulfilling legal, regulatory, accreditation, and reporting requirements in Hong Kong. 

System Administration and Improvement: Managing our IT systems, website, and online platforms, troubleshooting, data analysis, testing, research, statistical and survey purposes. 

We will only use your personal data for the purposes for which it was collected or for directly related purposes, unless we obtain your explicit consent or are otherwise permitted or required by law. 

 

#### 6. Disclosure and Transfer of Personal Data (Data Protection Principle 3) 

HKIIT may disclose or transfer your personal data to the following parties, both within and outside Hong Kong, where necessary for the purposes outlined in Section 5: 

Internal Departments: Relevant HKIIT departments, faculty, and staff who require access to the data to perform their duties. 

Government and Regulatory Bodies: Education Bureau, Immigration Department, Police Force, and other statutory or regulatory authorities as required by law. 

Accreditation Bodies: For quality assurance and accreditation purposes. 

Service Providers: Third-party vendors and service providers who assist us in delivering our services (e.g., IT support, payment processors, cloud hosting, event management, marketing agencies). These providers are contractually bound to protect your data and use it only for the purposes specified by HKIIT. 

Academic Partners: Other educational institutions for joint programs, student exchange, or verification of academic records. 

Parents/Guardians: For students who are minors, relevant information may be shared with their parents or legal guardians. 

Emergency Services: In situations where there is a risk to life, health, or safety. 

Alumni Associations: To facilitate alumni engagement and networking. 

Research Partners: For collaborative research projects, typically with anonymized or aggregated data. 

Transfer of Data Outside Hong Kong: In some cases, your personal data may be transferred to service providers or partners located outside of Hong Kong. When such transfers occur, HKIIT will take all reasonable and practicable steps to ensure that the data is protected to standards comparable to those under the PDPO, including using contractual clauses or ensuring the recipient jurisdiction has adequate data protection laws. 

 

#### 7. Data Security (Data Protection Principle 4) 

HKIIT implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard your personal data against unauthorized or accidental access, processing, erasure, loss, or use. These measures include: 

Access Controls: Restricting access to personal data to authorized personnel on a "need-to-know" basis. 

Encryption: Using encryption for sensitive data transmission and storage where appropriate. 

Physical Security: Securing physical records and access to Institute premises. 

Network Security: Implementing firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular security audits. 

Data Backup and Recovery: Regular backups to prevent data loss. 

Staff Training: Providing regular training to staff on data protection best practices and HKIIT's privacy policies. 

Data Minimisation: Collecting only the personal data that is necessary for the stated purposes. 

 

#### 8. Data Retention (Data Protection Principle 2) 

HKIIT will retain your personal data for no longer than is necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law, regulations, or HKIIT's internal policies. Different types of data may have different retention periods based on legal obligations (e.g., financial records, academic records) and operational needs. Once the retention period expires, personal data will be securely deleted or anonymized. 

 

#### 9. Your Rights as a Data Subject (Data Protection Principle 6) 

Under the PDPO, you have the following rights regarding your personal data held by HKIIT: 

Right of Access (Data Access Request - DAR): You have the right to request a copy of your personal data held by us. We will respond to your request within 40 days, as stipulated by the PDPO. A reasonable administrative fee may be charged for processing DARs. 

Right of Correction (Data Correction Request - DCR): You have the right to request the correction of any inaccurate personal data we hold about you. We will respond to your request within 40 days. 

Right to Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on your consent to process your personal data, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal. 

Right to Opt-Out of Direct Marketing: You have the right to opt-out of receiving direct marketing communications from us at any time. 

To exercise any of these rights, please refer to the "Contact Information" section below. 

 

#### 10. Use of Cookies and Other Tracking Technologies 

Our website and online platforms use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyze website traffic, and understand user behavior. 

What are Cookies? Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites that you visit. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. 

How We Use Cookies: 

Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential for the website to function correctly (e.g., session management, security). 

Performance Cookies: Collect anonymous information about how visitors use our website (e.g., pages visited, time spent). This helps us improve website functionality. 

Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and choices (e.g., language settings) to provide a more personalized experience. 

Targeting/Advertising Cookies (if applicable): Used to deliver relevant advertisements to you (only with your explicit consent). 

Managing Cookies: Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can choose to block or delete cookies, but this may affect the functionality of our website. For more information on how to manage cookies, please refer to your browser's help documentation.
